Skin Disease & Skin Cancer Information

What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are medical conditions that affect the skin’s structure, appearance, or function. They can be caused by infections, allergies, immune system disorders, environmental exposure, or genetic factors. Early detection is important to prevent complications.

Common Types of Skin Diseases

  • Melanoma: A dangerous form of skin cancer that develops from pigment-producing cells.
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma: Most common skin cancer, usually caused by sun exposure.
  • Actinic Keratosis: Rough, scaly patches caused by UV damage; can become cancerous.
  • Benign Keratosis: Non-cancerous growths common in aging skin.
  • Dermatofibroma: Small, firm, benign skin nodules.
  • Vascular Lesions: Abnormal blood vessel growths in the skin.

What is Skin Cancer?

Skin cancer occurs when skin cells grow abnormally, often due to excessive exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation. Early detection greatly increases survival rates.

Major Types of Skin Cancer

  • Melanoma: Spreads quickly; can be life-threatening.
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma: Slow-growing but can damage tissues.
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma: Can spread if untreated.
  • A — Asymmetry: One half unlike the other.
  • B — Border: Irregular, blurred edges.
  • C — Color: Multiple colors or uneven tone.
  • D — Diameter: Larger than 6mm.
  • E — Evolving: Changes in size, shape, or symptoms.

Prevention Tips

  • Use sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Avoid excessive sun exposure
  • Wear protective clothing
  • Regular skin self-examination
  • Consult a dermatologist for suspicious lesions
